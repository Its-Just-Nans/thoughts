The crème de la crème of English expression

---

I often read english articles and found some treasure in them, here is a non-exhausive list:

- [the cat's pyjamas](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/the_cat%27s_pyjamas): something outstanding, excellent, or highly admired
- [under the hood](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/under_the_hood): in its internal workings
- [top notch](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/top_notch): the highest quality
- [wishy-washy](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/wishy-washy): lacking in commitment
- [you can't have your cake and eat it too!](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/have_one%27s_cake_and_eat_it_too): enjoy two desirable but mutually exclusive things at the same time
- [cut your teeth on](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/cut_one%27s_teeth): to gain early experience
- [the lion's share](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/lion%27s_share):  the largest or best portion of something
- [the fat is in the fire](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/the_fat_is_in_the_fire): trouble will happen soon
- [in lieu of](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/in_lieu_of): instead of
- [under the weather](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/under_the_weather): feeling sick or unwell
- [hearing crickets](https://idioms.thefreedictionary.com/I+hear+crickets): complete silence or no response
- [the final nail in the coffin](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/final_nail_in_the_coffin): the last event or action that causes the failure of something that was already in decline or doomed
- [fell of my plate](https://www.clichelist.net/fell-off-my-plate/): having forgotten to do one of many things that you were supposed to do
- [the oldest trick in the book](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/the_oldest_trick_in_the_book): a way of tricking someone that is still effective although it has been used a lot
- [nothing new under the sun](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/there_is_nothing_new_under_the_sun): every new idea has some sort of precedent or echo from the past
- [cut from the same cloth](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/cut_from_the_same_cloth): very similar
- [the bee's knees](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/the_bee%27s_knees): something or someone excellent

See also [Anglish](https://anglish.org/wiki/Anglish) (english++)
